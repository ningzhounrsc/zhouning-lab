Research Focus

The functional connectivity of neuronal networks forms the fundamental basis for information processing in the brain. Our research group focuses on two key questions: how local neural circuits mediate animal cognition and behavior, and how neuropsychiatric disorders disrupt these functions at the cellular and network levels. We employ a multi-faceted approach combining in vivo two-photon calcium imaging, miniaturized endoscope imaging in freely moving animals, optogenetics/chemogenetics, and computational neural modeling. Our goal is to elucidate how population activity of neuronal ensembles encodes perception and memory related to behavior, and how alterations in local neural circuits are linked to brain disorders. Unraveling the mechanisms of neuronal population coding in brain diseases will provide crucial theoretical foundations for the development of novel therapeutic strategies.
